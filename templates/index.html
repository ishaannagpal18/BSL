BSL Mission

BSL- Brands and Sourcing Leaders Association, is a non-profit organization that represents Sourcing Leaders from the Domestic and Exports. We as a body have a comprehensive vision to bridge the gap between government policies and the apparel industry by guiding the policy makers about the advanced needs of the industry.

The association is formed to accelerate the growth of the Apparel Industry in the Country, and make the whole world wear “Make in India”.

About Us

NEWS STORY

WIDESPREAD OPPORTUNITIES IN THE INDIAN BAZAAR

India’s potential is unquestionable as India’s textiles sector is one of the oldest industries and it has a strong presence across the entire supply value chain.

Investments in this incredibly varied industry have expanded. India exported textiles worth $20.5 billion in 2020. $3.75 billion in foreign direct investment was made into the industry from April 2000 to December 2020. (FDI).

The industry has benefited from FDI and other other investments during the past five years. In May 2021, the ICIL announced an investment of $2.6 million. The Indian government has approved 100% automation under the automated technique.

The industry's output and exports are increasing thanks to additional investments made through initiatives like the Production Linked Incentive (PLI) and Scheme for Capital Building (SCBTS).

India's textile industry is expected to bring in investments of US$120 billion by 2025 and increase exports to US$ 300 billion

EXPORTS EXPANSION

Apparel exports recorded 30% increase in the FY22 to $16 billion compared to $12.3 billion in FY21. The exports of cotton yarns, fabrics, made-ups and handloom products rose by 55.11% to $15.244 billion in FY22. The export profits of Indian cotton yarn in 2021 were $4.69 billion i.e. 79.64% Year on Year growth rate. India accounts for 10.2% of the total global exports of raw cotton valued at $9.8 billion in FY22. As the core player in the regional economy, India is the gateway to South Asian markets ensuring the combined strength to create the second biggest textile and apparel industry in the world.

.01

DOMESTIC GROWTH

The Domestic market is charged up by its sizeable young population at 808 million youth below the age of 35, building India the 6th largest fashion bazaar across the globe. Apparel Industry has 73% share of the total T&A market as the young population adopts aspiration-based fashion instead of need-based clothing. India`s domestic textile and apparel market will reach $220 billion by 2025 from $100 billion in FY19. The booming $54 billion Indian fashion retail is estimated to reach $118 billion by 2028 while the $6.35 billion active wear market (2018) is estimated to reach $13.58 billion by 2024.

.02

RETAIL EXPANSION

The retail industry growth is expected 9% over 2019-2030, from $779 billion (2019) to $1.4 trillion (2026) and over $1.8 trillion by 2030. The Organized retail growth was commendable with 50% from 2012-2020, valued at 12% of total retail market. Major retail categories are apparel and accessories at 18% and home and living at 6%. The India`s e-retail market is expected to grow to $120-140 billion by FY26, at 25-30% annually over the next 5 years. India has the 3rd highest number of e-retail shoppers only behind China and the USA.

.03

FLOURISHING RAW MATERIAL IMPORTS OF INDIA

The demand of raw material and fabric is growing rapidly in the Indian market. There has been a phenomenal growth in Indian fashion retail, active wear, athleisure and women`s innerwear market which are valued at US$ 54 billion, $13.58 billion, $7.3 billion (2024) and $12 billion (2025) respectively induced by middle-class customers across the country.

Textile and Apparel consumption reached $75 billion in 2021 and is estimated to touch $190 billion by 2025. India is one of the most alluring consumer markets for apparel outside the West. Over 300 international fashion brands are expected to enter India in 2022-23 as per McKinsey. Imports into India are booming.
