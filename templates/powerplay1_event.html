B2B MARKETPLACESARE RAPIDLY EMERGING

BSL promosing a change in the procurement environment. This growth is making room for new competitors who want to overhaul supply chains.

Chief Guest Smt. Rachna Shah, IAS Textile Secretary, Ministry of Textiles, Government of India was felicitated by Padma Shri Dr. A. Sakthivel.

Brands and Sourcing Leaders Association (BSL) organized a grand event – Glocal Textile Convention to further the cause of founding principle “PANCH PAHAL”, at Stellar Resorts Rajokri-New Delhi.

The event was well supported by the esteemed Yarns, Fabrics and Accessories community, Smt. Rachna Shah shared her valuable experience and discussed huge opportunities for the Indian apparel Industry. She emphasized towards realizing the progressive trillion $ goal. The Indian textile industry excels at every stage of the value chain, from garments to home furnishings. She accentuated that construction of massive textile parks would be the best course of action in tandem with capacity building, cost engineering and adding smart factories.

An exhibition and conference were held during the event, giving attendees the chance to network, talk about, and present their platforms to the clothing and textile value chain showcasing latest trends and share in-depth expertise about their specialized disciplines.

The two-day event included the emphasis on sessions that highlighted the core areas -import substitution, export promotion, MMF + Technical textiles, cutting-edge technology, automation, digitalization and eco-friendly textiles, sustainability. The attendee at large had fashion buyers, apparel exporters, sourcing heads, and top industry leaders.

The community of Yarns, Fabrics and Accessories were conferred with recognition awards. A vote of thanks and appreciation from the entire garment fraternity to the dignitaries marked the conclusion of the convocation.

DAY 2 - DYNAMIC FORUM
"MMF 10X (MAN MADE FIBRES & TECHNICAL TEXTILES)"

As global fashion trends change, there is a growing demand for textiles composed of man-made fibers (MMF) as an alternative to cotton. With a 72:28 ratio, or 72% MMF and 28% natural fiber, MMF currently dominates the use of textile fiber globally. Due to the inherent growth restrictions of cotton and other natural fibers, the share of MMF has been gradually rising.

Given that future demand is likely to favor manufactured fiber-based textiles, as concluded, special attention is required to increase consumption and production.
